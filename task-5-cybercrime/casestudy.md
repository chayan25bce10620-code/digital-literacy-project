# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing forms of cybercrime in India, targeting college students, small business owners, and first-time digital payment users.

## How It Happens
A fraudster contacts the victim — typically via phone or WhatsApp — posing as a bank representative, online seller, or someone offering a refund or cashback. They send a "collect request" on the victim's UPI app, which looks identical to a payment confirmation screen. The victim, believing they are receiving money, enters their UPI PIN to "accept" it. In reality, they have just authorised a payment out of their own account. The money is transferred instantly and is nearlyimpossible to recover.

## Who Is Targeted
Young adults making their first online transactions, users of second-hand marketplaces like OLX, and anyone unfamiliar with the difference between a "send" and a "collect" request.

## Consequences
Victims lose money immediately with little legal recourse. Emotional consequences include distrust of digital payments and financial stress. In some reported cases, students lost their entire month's allowance in a single transaction.
